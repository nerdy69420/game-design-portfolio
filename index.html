Blade & Sorcery Mod

  • Back
  • ← Last
  • Next →

Blade & Sorcery is a VR fantasy sword fighting game developed by Warp Frog. One of the core gameplay features is the ability to climb any static object with a collider component. I noticed there weren't many maps that primarily focused on the climbing aspect of the game, so I put together a small map with many different things to climb.
The highlight of the map is an aircraft hangar outfitted with rock climbing walls and an indoor ropes course. Outside the hangar are a few areas with props I thought would be fun to climb on, such as shipping containers, broadcast towers, concrete tubes, and a couple other industrial-themed props.

Players can download the map from nexusmods.com and install it using the Vortex mod manager. Listed below are all 3d models and Unity packages utilized in the making of this map.

UE4 Cinematic Level

  • Back

School Project - Unreal Engine 4

  • Back

This is a project I created for one of my level design classes called Virtual Worlds. The purpose of making this level was to create a short, interactive experience similar to games like Dear Esther and The Stanley Parable. Some of the core aspects I focused on throughout the making of the level were music and SFX, lighting, and some basic animation. I composed all of the background music using Ableton Live and the BBC Symphony Orchestra Spitfire plugin.

In my level, the player starts in an old, run-down apartment located in a satellite state of the Soviet Union. After viewing a live broadcast on TV, the player learns the Berlin Wall has fallen and the Soviet Union is beginning to break apart. After exploring the apartment, a note can be found at the door from the player's friend Anton, inviting them to explore a nearby military installation that was recently abandonded by the consolidating Soviet armed forces. Once the player arrives, an automated missile launch system activates. The player must find a way to interrupt the launch sequence before the missile fires.

Listed first are two video play-throughs of the level. The first video is a full play-through that shows how to get the intended, "good ending" while the second video shows what happens if you get the "bad ending". I've included all the project files as well as a PDF which contains general documentation as well as highlighting the core functionality in the UE4 level blueprint. Lastly, I included all the music files I composed along with sheet music for the track that plays during the launch sequence.

About Me

Hey there! My name is Dominic Russo. I grew up in the sleepy, New England town of Wallingford, CT. At an early age, I would spend most of my time building lego sets and playing around on the family computer, a practice my parents begrudgingly accepted. My father would spark my interest in science, introducing me to the wonders of astronomy, physics, and chemistry just as I was getting a handle on the English language. The observatory behind me in the photo above was built by my dad in July of the year 2000 and houses a 12" Meade LX 200 Schmidt–Cassegrain telescope which we used to hunt for asteroids and other near-earth objects.

While attending grade school, I began taking karate lessons to learn basic self-defense and would eventually help teach newer students in lower belt levels. When I entered high school, I found excitement in both practical and creative activities outside of class. I was a member of the programming club, robotics team, and drama club all throughout my four years in high school, serving as Lighting Director for the stage crew in my senior year. I also developed a passion for esports that began when I co-founded the esports club and played main support for my high school's Overwatch team. In my freshman year I became the manager for RIT's Division 2 Overwatch team in my freshman year and then moved on to work with the Intramural Esports program for the remainder of my time on campus.

When it came time to choose which path I would pursue in college, I knew I wanted to make games. Ever since my parents introduced me to the family PC, I was enamored with the depth and diversity of the different virtual worlds people had created. I first began experimenting with Minecraft mods in early high school and soon would find my way to the Unity and Unreal Engines, where I spend most of my time today modding my favorite games.
